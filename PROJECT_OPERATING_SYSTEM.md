# Project Operating System

The core philosophy and decision framework for Rejct.all.
This is the document every other document answers to.

---

## 1. Position

Rejct.all is a **rejection system** expressed through clothing.

Clothing is the medium. The product is the position.

| It is                                | It is not                          |
| ------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------- |
| A position                           | A fashion brand                    |
| A rejection system                   | A style                            |
| Genderless                           | Unisex (merged) or androgynous     |
| Bilingual by design                  | Translated                         |
| Indian by default                    | "Indian-inspired"                  |
| Recognition                          | Aspiration                         |
| Self-alignment                       | Self-expression                    |
| Opting out without disappearing      | Opting out by going silent         |
| A new category                       | A niche within an existing one     |

---

## 2. Four pillars

Every decision is tested against these four.

### Pillar 1 — Refusal as the product
What we don't do, don't make, and don't say matters more than what we do.
- No restocks on past drops.
- No "fits" content.
- No influencer gifting.
- No discount codes.
- No participating in seasonal cycles (SS/FW, sale periods, festive drops).

### Pillar 2 — Bilingual by design
Hindi is not decoration. It carries meaning the English cannot.
- Every major release has a Hindi line that stands on its own.
- Hindi appears in Devanagari, not Roman transliteration.
- Hinglish is allowed in conversation, not in product/brand copy.

### Pillar 3 — Genderless, not neutral
Genderless is a stance, not a sizing chart. Fits, cuts, and copy are not about "for anyone" — they are about "not categorized."
- No "menswear" / "womenswear" split.
- Sizes are numeric or letter-based. No gendered terminology.
- Photography does not lean masculine or feminine as an aesthetic default.

### Pillar 4 — Internal alignment over external validation
We do not measure by reach, likes, or press hits. We measure by how close each piece and each word hews to the position.
- A drop that sells out but reads off-position is a loss.
- A drop that sells slowly but pulls in aligned people is a win.

## 3. Decision framework

When making a call — product, copy, channel, collab, hire — run it through this:

1. **Does this make Rejct.all more of a rejection system, or less?**
   If less, stop.

2. **Would the audience recognize themselves in this, or feel sold to?**
   Recognition wins. Aspiration fails.

3. **Could a trend-chasing brand have said or done this?**
   If yes, rewrite.

4. **Does this require explanation?**
   If the piece needs a caption to justify it, the piece is wrong — not the caption.

5. **Is there a terser version?**
   There almost always is.

---

## 4. What we are building (order of priority)

1. **The position** — documented, consistent, uncompromised.
2. **The product** — clothing that carries the position without a label or a caption explaining it.
3. **The early adopters** — small, aligned, loud in their own lives, quiet online.
4. **The category** — over years, not months.

Growth is an outcome. Not a goal.

---

## 5. Anti-goals

Things we actively will not chase, even if offered:

- Fashion-week slots or editorial placements that require diluting the position.
- "Founder story" content that centers a person over the system.
- Collabs with personalities who represent the culture we reject.
- Paid ads that optimize for conversion over fit.
- Marketplace listings (Myntra, Ajio, Amazon). Direct or not at all.
- Celebrity seeding.
- Drops timed to festivals, Diwali sales, Republic Day, or Valentine's.

## 6. Metrics that matter

| Track                                   | Ignore                         |
| --------------------------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| Repeat buyer rate                       | First-time conversion rate     |
| Unprompted mentions by aligned people   | Follower count                 |
| Depth of waitlist (intent signals)      | Email list size                |
| Time spent on product pages             | Bounce rate                    |
| Return rate (lower = better fit)        | Average order value            |
| How often a drop sells through without promotion | Social engagement rate |

---

## 7. How the OS is updated

This document changes slowly. Revisions require:
- A clear argument for why a pillar or anti-goal is wrong.
- A written proposal logged in `docs/decisions/`.
- A cooling-off period of at least 7 days before committing.

Tactics change fast. The OS does not.
