# Project Operating System

The operating system for `rejct.all`. **`Brand Bible.md` is the source of truth.** This document translates the Bible into operating rules. Anything here that contradicts the Bible is wrong — update this document, not the Bible.

---

## 1. Position (from Bible §02)

`rejct.all` — always lowercase. Founded Delhi, 2026. Genderless streetwear / contemporary fashion. Gen Z and Gen Alpha, both, simultaneously. India-first, global by default. Direct-to-consumer, Instagram primary, online-only at launch. Hindi and English — equal weight, neither a translation.

**Positioning:** editorial restraint with Gen Z energy. Indian, not aspirationally Western.

**What it is not:** streetwear that performs Indianness. A Western brand with Hindi overlay. Trying to be anything.

---

## 2. The Six Rejections (from Bible §03 — these are the pillars)

Every product, post, line of copy, partnership, and decision is tested against these.

1. **Reject the trend.** *ट्रेंड को नकारो।* — The drop exists because the design is right, not because it is timely.
2. **Reject the algorithm.** *एल्गोरिदम को नकारो।* — Built for the person who recognises themselves, not for reach.
3. **Reject performing.** *दिखावे को नकारो।* — The clothes are not a costume. The embroidery is quiet. The statement is loud.
4. **Reject the gaze.** *नज़र को नकारो।* — Decisions made in private, worn in public. No approval-seeking.
5. **Reject the label.** *लेबल को नकारो।* — Men's, women's, Indian, Western — every label is a box.
6. **Reject everything that isn't you.** *वो सब नकारो जो तुम नहीं हो।* — The final, most personal one.

---

## 3. Decision framework

Before shipping any decision (product, copy, channel, partner, hire):

1. **Does it serve at least one rejection without violating another?**
2. **Would the audience recognise themselves, or feel sold to?** Recognition passes; aspiration fails.
3. **Could a trend-chasing brand have said this?** If yes, rewrite.
4. **Does it require explanation?** If the piece needs a caption to justify itself, the piece is wrong — not the caption.
5. **Is there a terser version?** Almost always.

## 4. Hard rules (Bible-locked — do not change without updating the Bible)

### Naming & identity
- Brand name: `rejct.all` — always lowercase.
- Monogram: `R.` — serif, always with the period.
- Collection system: Volumes. Drop 01 = Volume 00 (Before). Drop 02 = Volume 01 (After).
- Drop numbering sequential and permanent. No Drop 01.5.

### Drop construction
- Min 4, max 8 styles per drop.
- At least one tonal piece per drop.
- At least one piece where the back is the hero.
- `R.` monogram present in every drop — the constant.
- No restocks. Ever.

### Design codes (established in Drop 01, govern future)
Off-white base. Wine red (Burgundy, Pantone 7421 C) primary embroidery. Tone-on-tone as highest expression. `REJECT` on sleeveless collars. Puff print for `R.` monogram. Angel seal as founding image. Script lettering as back hero. Graphic as statement, never decoration.

### Sizing
XS — XXL. One genderless size system. No men's/women's labelling.

### Commerce
- Website: `rejctall.com`. Instagram: `@rejct.all`.
- Online-only at launch. No retail. No marketplace listings.
- No sale pricing, discount codes, or promotional pricing. Ever.
- Tonal colourway ≥ price of the equivalent graphic colourway.
- Free shipping above ₹3,999. Flat ₹149 below.
- No COD. Prepaid only.

### Voice
- Direct, bilingual by default, never ironic, terse, never aspirational.
- Banned hype words: `drop` (as hype), `collab`, `cop`, `fire`, `goes hard`. (`Drop` as the Bible's noun — `Drop 01` — is allowed.)
- No fabric specifications (`GSM`, etc.) in customer-facing copy.

## 5. Anti-goals

Things actively refused even if offered:

- Paid placements, sponsored posts, advertorials.
- Marketplace listings (Myntra, Ajio, Amazon).
- Celebrity gifting for reach.
- Influencer seeding for reach (see Bible §10 Phase 4 — real people wearing it is the model, not paid creators).
- Discount-led sales moments (festive, end-of-season, Black Friday).
- Founder-personality content centering a person over the brand.
- Hype cycles (countdown timers, "3 days to go" posts, FOMO urgency).
- Marketplace-style product language (GSM, "premium quality," spec sheets).

**Not refused (per Bible roadmap Q3 2026):** one carefully chosen collaboration, PR seeding to selected individuals (no paid placement), and written editorial placements if aligned.

## 6. Metrics that matter

| Track | Ignore |
| - | - |
| Drop 01 sell-through without paid promotion | First-time conversion rate |
| Repeat buyers from Drop 01 into Volume 01 | Follower count |
| Unprompted wearer posts (real people) | Engagement rate |
| Return rate (lower = better fit) | Average order value |
| Depth of intent signals before a drop | Email list size |

Growth is an outcome. Not a goal.

## 7. How this OS is updated

The Brand Bible is versioned (currently v2.0). This document changes when:
- The Bible changes.
- A playbook outcome contradicts an operating rule here and a new rule must be written.

Changes require a decision record in `docs/decisions/` and a 7-day cooling-off period. Never silently contradict the Bible.
